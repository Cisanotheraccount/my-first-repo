01

JUL 2016 / BASELINE

Five routes depart from very different price points.

Manhattan begins at $3,400. The Bronx begins at exactly half that amount, while Brooklyn, Queens, and Staten Island form the middle of the system.

02

2020 / INVENTORY SHOCK

The pandemic sends available listings sharply upward.

Manhattan inventory peaks above 40,000 listings in August 2020. Brooklyn reaches its own decade high one month later.

03

2021 / RENT TROUGH

More choice briefly pushes the central lines downward.

Manhattan falls to $2,750 in January 2021. Queens reaches $2,000 in the same month; Brooklyn bottoms shortly afterward.

04

2022 / RAPID REBOUND

The discount era closes almost as quickly as it opened.

As inventory contracts, asking rents accelerate past their pre-pandemic levels across Manhattan, Brooklyn, and Queens.

05

2023 / 1.41% VACANCY

The official vacancy survey marks an exceptionally tight city.

NYC's net rental vacancy rate drops from 4.54% in 2021 to 1.41% in 2023, one of the lowest readings since the survey began.

06

JUN 2026 / NEW TERMINUS

Every borough arrives higher than where it started.

The Bronx records the largest nominal increase at 76.2%. Manhattan remains the most expensive line at $4,965.

Reflection

A temporal structure shows when change happens. A relational structure shows how parts of the system connect. For my project interests, this method can map relationships between urban systems, media tools, design decisions, and spatial experience instead of reading each element as an isolated object.

Methodology / Sources

What these lines measure—and what they do not.

01 / MARKET DATA

Monthly median asking rent and rental inventory come from the StreetEasy Data Dashboard master download. Asking rent is the exact middle asking rent among listings available on StreetEasy at any point in the month. Inventory is the number of listings available at any point in that month.

02 / OFFICIAL CHECKPOINTS

Net rental vacancy rates come from the NYC Department of Housing Preservation and Development's New York City Housing and Vacancy Survey.

03 / LIMITS

Asking rents are not the same as rents paid by all current tenants. Values are nominal dollars and are not inflation-adjusted. Platform coverage is thinner in Staten Island and the Bronx, so changes there can be more volatile.

04 / SNAPSHOT

The page uses a fixed, locally stored snapshot covering July 2016 through June 2026. This keeps the analysis consistent and reproducible across visits.
